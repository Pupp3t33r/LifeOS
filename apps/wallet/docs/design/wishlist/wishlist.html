wallet · Wishlist — the wants backlog (Money ADR-0022 · 0034 · 0019, amended)

Everything you're wishing for

A flat list of the things you want — the backlog Plan · Board pulls from but never let you edit. Here you add, price, and remove wants. Each row wears its schedule as month chips — a One-time want shows the one month it's planned (or that it's paid off), and a Repeat want shows every month it's restocked.

One chip language for every want — no commitment track. Each row carries a coloured stage dot (clay = Planned, denim = Paying off, sage = Bought, hollow = Wishing) plus its schedule as month chips. Chips show current + future only — paid history lives in Activity, so they never accumulate, and no year is shown. A chip is outline when unpaid, filled ✓ when paid. ×N on a chip is the item count for that month (two packs of tea in Oct → Oct ×2). Status is read-only here: Idle · Planned · Financed · Bought is the derived projection (ADR-0034) — moved by planning on the Board or buying on Home, never hand-edited. Bought wants hide in the collapsed row; the default view is Active. [units] A want's quantity is stored as a generic dimension — Pieces · Mass · Volume · Length — and the symbol is cosmetic, rendered from the user's metric/imperial preference (kg↔lb, L↔gal, m↔ft): switching it relabels without converting, and the backend never sees kg/lb. Pieces hide their symbol (×2); the others show it (×0.5 kg). Category is on the want (amends ADR-0022/0034): pick from user categories; the system/domain ones (Books · Board Games · Video Games) arrive with linking. Nav: Wishlist has its own bottom-bar slot (amends Wallet ADR-0002/0005). Removing a committed want does not cancel its planned buy — the line keeps tracking until cleared on the Board; nothing's paid, so no money is affected.
