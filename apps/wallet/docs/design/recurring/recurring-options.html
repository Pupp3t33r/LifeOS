Recurring, in the Home worklist

A recurring item is not a new kind of thing on screen — it's an Upcoming row you tick off, exactly like a planned purchase. Same checkbox, same "Mark paid," same Upcoming→Logged move. Rent −$1,000 on the 1st, Utilities ~−$200 on the 15th, Salary +$4,200 on the 25th, plus a Laptop installment. Marking paid logs the planned amount in one tap. For an Ongoing item you can open the row to log a different real amount; a Payment plan installment (the Laptop) pays exactly as scheduled — one tap, no override.

Reconciled with your notes: Plan tab untouched. No separate "due" colour or icon — an item is just unticked (Upcoming) or ticked (Logged); "due Jul 1" is only a date. Creating a recurring is its own dialog from the Home FAB — see create-options.html. All vocabulary borrowed from cockpit-final.html.

Appear & mark paid

Recurring items land in Upcoming with an empty checkbox. Tap Mark paid (or the checkbox) to log them as planned — they move to Logged, ticked. The date sits inline as a plain label; the row that's past its date just reads its date in clay.

Why: nothing new to learn — it's the planned-purchase interaction you already designed. Income (Salary) uses the same row with a + amount. "Due" was never a state; it's just an unticked row in the period you're looking at.

Override is Ongoing-only: tapping the row body to log a different real amount applies to Ongoing items (Rent, Utilities). A Payment plan installment (Laptop) records its scheduled slice — Mark paid is the whole interaction, and its row body doesn't open an amount sheet (ADR-0028).
