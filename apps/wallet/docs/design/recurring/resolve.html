Mark paid — plan vs. real

Two paths, by frequency. Paid as planned (the common case): one tap on Mark paid / the checkbox — logs the planned amount, done, no sheet. Different real amount: tap the row to open the sheet pre-filled, type the real figure. Utilities planned −$200, bill came to −$180 → open, type 180. The plan stays $200; only this month records $180.

Per your notes: no "just this month" warning, and the plan-vs-real cue is kept light — the logged row shows planned −$200 as a faint strikethrough only when the two differ. If even that reads as too much, it can go — the real number alone is enough.

Two paths

One tap, or open

Paid exactly as planned? Mark paid on the row logs it — no sheet at all. Different real amount? Tap the row body to open the sheet, pre-filled to the plan; overwrite the figure and confirm.

The mechanism: the sheet's amount field seeds from the plan and you can overwrite it. Confirming records the real amount as a flow on this period; the plan is never touched. That's the API's optional line-override — surfaced only when you actually need it, not on every confirm.
