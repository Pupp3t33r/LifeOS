Home — three directions for the savings canvas

Same month, same numbers (June 2026 · +$4,200 in, $2,650 out, $300 planned → projected $1,250 against a $1,000 target). What changes is the form of the hero. Each shown on desktop (nav rail) and phone (bottom bar). All built from the existing Calm tokens — bone, sage, clay, Bricolage Grotesque.

A · Ledger

The honest ledger

The onboarding canvas, grown up. A single document that adds money in, takes money out, and resolves to one line: on track by +$250.

Why it fits: "the math is honest" is the product's first principle. Nothing is hidden behind a gauge. Lowest risk, highest legibility, continuous with what users already met at onboarding.

B · Verdict

Verdict first

Lead with the only number that carries feeling — are you ahead or short of your target? — over a single target-vs-projected bar. The arithmetic shrinks to a strip beneath.

Why it fits: answers "am I okay this month?" before you read a single row. The signature is the overshoot bar (projected past the target marker), not a generic big-number-with-gradient. Risk: the verdict can feel like a dashboard if the copy isn't honest.

C · Waterfall

The cascade

The canvas made spatial. Income falls through spending and planned purchases and pools as savings. The target is a waterline you drag; planned purchases visibly lower the pool.

Why it's the risk: it's the one view that makes the PLAN's own metaphor literal — "drop an $800 lens into the month and watch savings fall." Most memorable, most work, and the bars must stay honest at small amounts.
