Plan — a home for what you've committed to

Home is the current-period cockpit: it deals in instances — this month's rows, which you log and mark paid. What it has no room for is the definitions behind them: the Ongoing items, the Payment plans, the things you mean to buy. Editing one today means hunting for its instance on the right month. Plan is where the definitions live — period-agnostic, editable, and forward-looking. Three ways it could work, on the same axis we keep circling: object · time · money.

Revisits a settled call. The Home pass dissolved Plan as a destination (home/README.md §3) and gave the slot to Wishlist. These directions fold the wishlist / planned-purchase idea into Plan instead, so one tab owns everything forward-looking. Model guardrails held throughout: a Payment plan is immutable except Cancel (ADR-0028) — so its row opens Manage, never a field edit; a planned purchase is period-local (ADR-0018), shown under the period it's filed on. Nav shown as Home · Plan · Activity · Accounts to match the current app shell.

A · Object-first

The library

“What am I committed to?”

A grouped shelf of your durable objects — Ongoing, Payment plans, Planned purchases — each a row you open to manage. Every object carries its footprint: what's left, when it's next due. Create lives here too, one New per shelf.

Why it fits: it's the literal answer to the missing screen — the place a definition lives when it isn't an instance on any one month. Closest to how the objects already model.

Watch: it's a list of lists. Reads as “settings” unless the footprints earn their keep. A plan row is Manage (progress + Cancel), not Edit — the model won't let you rewrite terms.

B · Time-first

The horizon

“When does it hurt?”

Plan as a forward run of months — the inverse of Home's single-period view. Each month shows its committed outflow as a bar, so a heavy month reads at a glance. Pick a month to see what lands; tap any row to open its definition.

Why it fits: definitions matter because of when they bite. A timeline turns “I have a car loan” into “October is $2,210 — the tires land on top of everything.” Planned buys get a natural home: a future month.

Watch: the boldest, least conventional. Editing is one hop deeper (month → row → definition). Needs the bar to be honest, not decorative — it's the whole signature.

C · Money-first

The dials

“What should this month add up to?”

Plan as the lever room behind Home's on-track strip. Set a budget per category and a savings target; a live projected month recomputes as you move a dial. The commitments that feed it sit underneath, read-only.

Why it fits: Home's budget strip already links to a “Manage” that has nowhere to go. This is that destination — the place you shape the month the cockpit then reports against.

Watch: the least about editing objects (they're mostly read-only here) and the most net-new surface to build — budgets and the projection engine (MonthProjection) aren't shipped yet. Furthest from today's model.
